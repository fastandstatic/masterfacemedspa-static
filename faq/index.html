FAQ.

01. What should I expect during my first visit to Master Face Med Spa?

During your first visit, you’ll likely fill out some paperwork and have a consultation with one of our Medical providers for a facial evaluation and to discuss your goals and any health concerns. They’ll recommend treatments or treatment plans based on your needs and preferences.

02. It is possible to add additional services to my appointment?

If you’re interested in adding additional treatments to your initial injectable session at Master Face Med Spa, simply let your medical provider know. Depending on the availability of the provider, it may be possible to add on the treatments during the same session. However, some treatments may require more time for a thorough consultation and procedure. If the time window and availability of the provider do not allow for the additional treatments, we will schedule a new appointment for you at a time that is convenient. The cost of any additional treatments will be paid at the time of sale. Don’t hesitate to ask us about adding on treatments – we’re here to help you achieve your aesthetic goals.

09. Is it Botox (neurotoxin) A filler?

The main difference between a neurotoxin and a filler is the way they work.

Neurotoxins, such as Botox, Dysport, and Xeomin, are made from a purified form of botulinum toxin and work by temporarily paralyzing the muscles that cause wrinkles. They are typically used to treat dynamic wrinkles, which are caused by repetitive muscle movements, such as frown lines, crow’s

feet, and forehead wrinkles. Neurotoxins are injected directly into the muscle, and the effects can last for several months.

Fillers, on the other hand, are substances that are injected into the skin to add volume and fill in wrinkles or lines. They are typically made from hyaluronic acid, a naturally occurring substance in the body that helps to hydrate and plump the skin. Fillers are used to treat static wrinkles, which are caused by a loss of volume or elasticity in the skin, such as nasolabial folds, marionette lines, and hollow cheeks. The effects of fillers can last anywhere from several months to a year or more, depending on the type of filler used.

In summary, neurotoxins work by temporarily paralyzing the muscles that cause wrinkles, while fillers work by adding volume to the skin to fill in wrinkles or lines.

10. How long do treatments (injectables) normally last and how often should I make a maintenance appointment

At Master Face Med Spa we offer temporary aesthetic treatments that are non-invasive, non-permanent, and more affordable than traditional cosmetic surgeries. The frequency of maintenance treatments will depend on the specific procedure you received. Typically, our injectable treatments last between 6 and 24 months, but the duration of effects can vary from patient to patient based on factors such as activity levels, metabolism, and genetics. To help maintain your results, it’s important to keep your skin healthy and hydrated. Drinking plenty of water can help keep your skin hydrated and improve the effectiveness of hyaluronic acid fillers. Our medical providers can also recommends skincare products that will complement the effects of your treatment. For more information on maintenance guidelines for specific treatments, please visit the relevant pages on our website.

11. It is normal to experience bruising and swelling after my filler treatment?

It’s common to experience some swelling and bruising after your treatment, but don’t worry – this is usually temporary and should subside within a few days. To help minimize bruising, we recommend avoiding aspirin and alcohol for 24 hours before and after your appointment. If you do experience bruising, you can apply arnica cream and take Arnica pills to help speed up the healing process. To reduce swelling, you can gently ice the treated area for 12-24 hours after your treatment. It’s also important to stay hydrated by drinking plenty of fluids, ideally around 2-3 liter of water per day. If you notice excessive swelling or bruising that lasts for more than 14 days, or if you have any other concerns about your treatment, please don’t hesitate to contact us at 7864614665 or reach out to your local Master Face Med Spa to speak to a medical provider. We’re here to support you every step of the way.

12. What should I do to best prepare for my aesthetic treatment?

To prepare for your treatment at Master face Med Spa, your doctor will discuss any questions you have about aftercare and provide you with general guidelines to follow before your appointment. To reduce the risk of infection and minimize swelling and bruising, we recommend avoiding alcohol

consumption for at least 24 hours before and after your treatment. It’s also important to avoid taking aspirin or other anti-inflammatory drugs such as Ibuprofen for up to 24 hours before your appointment. Additionally, we advise against undergoing minimally invasive skin care treatments such as micro-needling, chemical peels, or laser treatments leading up to your appointment. For more specific guidelines, please visit the relevant treatment pages on our website. Our goal is to ensure that your appointment is as comfortable and relaxed as possible, and we’re here to support you every step of the way.

13. The after-care process.

The treatment aftercare process will vary depending on the specific treatment you receive at Sisu Clinic. Your doctor will provide you with detailed aftercare instructions during your appointment. However, there are some general recommendations that apply to all treatments to ensure a fast healing process and the best possible results. To minimize the risk of infection, we recommend avoiding alcohol consumption for at least 24 hours before and after your treatment. It’s also important to avoid wearing makeup for at least 12 hours after your appointment and to keep the treated area free of bacteria. Avoid facials, saunas, and steam rooms for 72 hours following your treatment. After your treatment, aim to drink at least 2-3L of water per day to stay hydrated. Our doctors also recommend avoiding vigorous exercise for 24 hours after your non-invasive treatment. For more specific guidelines on treatment aftercare, please visit the relevant treatment pages on our website. We’re here to support you every step of the way and ensure that your recovery process is as smooth and comfortable as possible.
